# Appraisal Dimensions

Walk **every** dimension below on each run. Each maps to a concern code (see `concern_codes_and_severity.md`). For each dimension record: **met / partially met / not met**, the **evidence** seen in V1, and — when not fully met — an **issue** with a required action for V2.

These dimensions are the operational form of `Yassin_PFE_01_Review_V1`'s own "Critical appraisal mode" and "Quality gates". **Under the authored standards (R1/R5/R6): the article is a STANDALONE systematic review; accept the forced systematic review type (never score the scoping→systematic override a mismatch), expect worldwide subject-driven inclusion, and treat the Component-2 dimensions below (PFE, FEAS) as SUSPENDED.** Do not invent dimensions outside this set; if something genuinely falls outside, record it as an `Observation`.

---

## 1. Question and framework

- **QUES — Review question clarity.** Met when the question is precise and answerable. Defects: vague topic, no defined concept/outcome, scope creep.
- **FRAM — Framework fit.** Met when the synthesis framework matches the question type and is justified. The review type is a **systematic** synthesis (R5); the scoping→systematic override is legitimate and is **never** a FRAM/PRISMA mismatch. Defects: no framework; framework mismatched to the question.

## 2. Review type and reporting standard

- **PRISMA — Reporting-standard compliance.** Met when the correct standard is applied (PRISMA 2020 / PRISMA-P / PRISMA-S / PRISMA-ScR / PRISMA-DTA) and a flow is reported. Defects: wrong standard for the type; **invented PRISMA counts** (Critical); no flow narrative.
- **STRUCT — Structure completeness.** Met when the manuscript/preliminary structure is complete (V1 lists both). Defects: missing Methods subsections, absent required tables A–I, no certainty section.

## 3. Search and sourcing

- **SRCH — Search depth and reproducibility.** Met when the Consensus deep cycle (or a justified subset) was run and is reproducible from the log (Table A). Defects: **no documented search** (Critical), **Consensus skipped when available** (Critical), missing query records, no contradictory-evidence sweep.
- **TRIA — Database triangulation.** Met when sources beyond Consensus were used or their absence justified, with per-source reporting (Table B). Defects: single-source reliance presented as a full systematic search.

## 4. Selection and evidence handling

- **ELIG — Eligibility criteria.** Met when explicit, pre-specified inclusion/exclusion with reasons, plus PFE feasibility criteria. Defects: criteria absent, post-hoc, or not applied consistently.
- **CIT — Citation verification.** Met when included studies are verified (Table I) and unverified items are excluded or clearly labelled. Defects: **unverified citations used as core evidence** (Critical), fabricated identifiers, citation–claim mismatch.
- **EXTR — Extraction quality.** Met when extraction is study-level across the required fields (Table D), not abstract-only without saying so. Defects: shallow extraction, missing limitations fields.

## 5. Appraisal and synthesis

- **METHCOMP — Methodological comparison.** Met when studies are compared on design, sampling, instruments, analysis, transferability (Table E) **before** findings are synthesised. Defects: studies merely listed; no comparison (Major).
- **HETERO — Heterogeneity analysis.** Met when methodological/clinical/contextual/outcome (and statistical, if meta-analytic) heterogeneity is assessed and used to justify the synthesis method (Table F). Defects: heterogeneity ignored; synthesis method unjustified.
- **ROB — Risk of bias / quality.** Met when a design-appropriate tool (RoB 2, ROBINS-I/E, NOS, JBI, AXIS, MMAT, QUADAS-2, QUIPS, COSMIN, AMSTAR 2/ROBIS) is applied with judgement, concerns, and **consequence for synthesis** (Table G). Defects: no tool; bare "good/moderate" labels; no consequence stated.
- **SYNTH — Synthesis quality.** Met when the synthesis integrates rather than lists, answering V1's nine synthesis questions (what is established, uncertain, consistent/contradictory, underrepresented, dominant tools, recurring weaknesses, why methods explain differences, specific gaps, implications). Defects: study-by-study paraphrase; **incompatible studies merged as equivalent** (Critical); **meta-analysis on non-comparable data** (Critical).
- **CERT — Certainty / confidence.** Met when GRADE / GRADE-CERQual or a structured confidence assessment is provided. Defects: certainty absent; confidence asserted without basis.

## 6. Interpretation and gaps

- **INTERP — Proportionality of claims.** Met when claims about practice/policy/education/clinic are proportionate to certainty and directness. Defects: **overstated implications** (Critical when clinical/safety), interpretation not tied to the evidence, **contradictory evidence ignored** (Critical).
- **GAP — Evidence-gap specificity.** Met when gaps state *what, where, for whom, and why*. Defects: generic "more research is needed" (Major).
- **ORIG — Originality / duplication risk.** Met when the review's contribution over existing reviews is articulated. Defects: duplicates an existing review without justification.

## 7. ESSS PFE fit — SUSPENDED under R1 (standalone article)

**The review is a standalone, publishable article (R1).** Do NOT raise `PFE` or `FEAS` issues, and do not require a Component-2 bridge or empirical feasibility. `ETH` and `CTX` apply only at the literature/review level (ethics reported in the evidence; contextual transferability in the Discussion), never as empirical-design requirements. (Dimensions retained below for reference only.)

- **PFE — Component-2 implications.** Met when the review yields explicit, feasible empirical-design implications (variables, instruments, population, sampling, analysis) for the empirical study. Defects: no bridge to Component 2; implications not feasible.
- **FEAS — Feasibility for an ESSS student.** Met when implied next steps are achievable within a 3–4 month Licence-level project. Defects: designs needing inaccessible clinical access or unlikely permissions.
- **ETH — Ethical acceptability.** Met when implied empirical directions are non-invasive and ethically realistic. Defects: sensitive/accusatory or high-risk designs implied.
- **CTX — Contextual relevance.** Met when Moroccan / North African / Francophone / institutional relevance is addressed where pertinent. Defects: context absent where it materially matters.

## 8. Publication targeting

- **JTGT — Journal-target fit and publication potential.** Met when the report's emphasis suits a plausible target journal family (or a general high-quality structure if none is set). Defects: mismatch between content and any stated target; no realistic publication pathway. (Note: detailed journal selection is Step 10, `Yassin_PFE_10_Publishing_Assistance`; here, only flag fit problems that V2 should pre-empt.)
