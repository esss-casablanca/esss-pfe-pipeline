# Journal-Selection Criteria (standard set)

Score each finalist 1–5 on each criterion (5 = best fit for this student and this review). Record
the evidence and its source for every score. Then rank by the weighted total, but let scope/fit and
ethics act as gates: a journal that fails scope/fit or legitimacy is not recommended regardless of
its other scores.

| Criterion | What to assess | Verify from |
|---|---|---|
| **Scope/fit** (gate) | Do the journal's aims and accepted article types include this review type and topic? | Journal "Aims & Scope" and recent issues |
| **Indexing** | PubMed/MEDLINE, Scopus, Web of Science, DOAJ; coverage relevant to the field | Journal site + the index's own database |
| **APC / open-access** | Fee amount, OA vs subscription, waivers for low/middle-income authors | Journal "APC"/"Open access" page |
| **Timeline** | Typical time to first decision and to publication | Journal stats page or recent author reports |
| **Impact factor / quartile** | JCR Impact Factor and/or Scopus CiteScore / SJR quartile | JCR / Scopus Sources / Scimago |
| **Ethics / legitimacy** (gate) | Peer-reviewed; transparent editorial process; COPE membership or equivalent; not predatory | COPE, DOAJ, journal policies; absence from credible predatory lists |

## Matching ambition to the work
Be honest and kind. A rigorous Licence-level review may be better served by a reputable Q3/Q4 or a
solid indexed open-access journal than by a top-quartile venue with a high rejection rate and long
timeline. Recommend a realistic spread: typically one ambitious-but-plausible target, one strong
core fit, and one safer/faster option — and say which is which.

## Legitimacy screen (part of the ethics criterion)
Confirm the journal is genuinely indexed and peer-reviewed before recommending it. Treat as warning
signs: guaranteed rapid acceptance, no verifiable indexing, opaque or absent peer review, spoofed
metrics, or aggressive solicitation. If a journal cannot be verified, do not recommend it.
