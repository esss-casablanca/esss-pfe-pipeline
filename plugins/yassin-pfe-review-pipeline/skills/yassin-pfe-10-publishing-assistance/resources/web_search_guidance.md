# Web-Search Guidance (journals & current author instructions)

Guidelines and metrics change, so search **live**; do not rely on memory.

## Finding candidates
- Search the review's topic + type + field (e.g. "scoping review nursing education journal").
- Consider field-appropriate indexed journals; build a pool of 6–10, then narrow to three finalists.
- Use journal-finder style queries cautiously and verify everything against primary sources.

## Verifying each finalist (cite every fact)
- **Scope & article types** — the journal's official Aims & Scope and recent issues.
- **Indexing** — confirm on the index's own site (PubMed/MEDLINE, Scopus Sources, Web of Science,
  DOAJ), not only the journal's claim.
- **APC / OA** — the journal's fees/OA page; note waivers.
- **Timeline** — journal statistics or credible author-reported data.
- **Metrics** — JCR Impact Factor and/or Scopus CiteScore / Scimago SJR quartile.
- **Legitimacy** — COPE membership, transparent peer review; check against credible predatory-journal
  warnings.

## Rules
- Attribute every journal fact to its source.
- Do **not** reproduce author-guideline text verbatim — paraphrase and apply it.
- If a fact cannot be verified from a credible source, say so rather than guessing; never fabricate a
  metric, fee, or scope statement.
