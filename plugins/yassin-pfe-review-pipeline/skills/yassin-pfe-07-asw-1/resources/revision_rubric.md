# Revision Rubric

Use the minimum sufficient intervention level.

| Level | Name | Use when | Allowed actions | Meaning-drift risk |
|---:|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | No revision | Text is already precise and coherent | No change or brief confirmation | None |
| 1 | Editorial correction | Grammar, spelling, punctuation, minor clarity | Light copyediting | Very low |
| 2 | Academic style revision | Text is understandable but generic, repetitive, or weakly structured | Improve flow, terminology, paragraph logic | Low |
| 3 | Scholarly strengthening | Claims are vague, insufficiently specific, or weakly linked to method/data/literature | Add specificity from supplied information, mark missing evidence | Moderate |
| 4 | Methodological/evidence reconstruction | Methods, data links, or argument structure are incomplete | Rebuild logic using supplied details; insert markers for gaps | High |
| 5 | Not safely revisable | Critical information is absent or identifiable/sensitive material is unresolved | Do not finalize; produce author checklist and diagnostic comments | Very high |

## Calibration

### Level 1 example

Original: “The study was conducted in Casablanca and data was collected by questionnaire.”
Revision: “The study was conducted in Casablanca, and data were collected using a questionnaire.”

### Level 2 example

Original: “This topic is very important because many students have problems.”
Revision: “This topic is relevant because it concerns recurrent difficulties reported among students in academic and clinical training environments.”

### Level 3 example

Original: “The results show that students face many barriers.”
Revision: “The results indicate that students reported barriers related to [DATA REQUIRED], suggesting that the issue cannot be reduced to individual motivation alone.”

### Level 4 example

Original: “A questionnaire was used and the results were analyzed.”
Revision: “Data were collected using a [METHOD DETAIL REQUIRED] questionnaire administered to [METHOD DETAIL REQUIRED] participants. The analysis should specify the variables examined, the statistical approach, and the criteria used to interpret the findings. [METHOD DETAIL REQUIRED]”

### Level 5 example

Original: “The intervention improved patients’ recovery by 70%.”
Action: Do not revise as a fact unless the dataset, outcome definition, denominator, statistical method, and source table are supplied. Mark `[DATA REQUIRED]`.

## Escalation rules

- Move from Level 2 to Level 3 when scientific claims are vague or unsupported.
- Move from Level 3 to Level 4 when methods, data, or references are structurally missing.
- Move to Level 5 when revision would require inventing evidence.
