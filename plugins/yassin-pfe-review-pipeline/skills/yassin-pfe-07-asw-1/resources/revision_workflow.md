# Expanded Revision Workflow

## Step 1 — Intake

Identify:

- document language;
- document type;
- health-science field;
- target audience;
- requested revision scope;
- availability of Skill 1 audit output;
- availability of data, tables, references, or methods details;
- confidentiality risks.

## Step 2 — Determine revision scope

Use the smallest scope adequate to the task:

- sentence-level correction;
- paragraph-level revision;
- section-level revision;
- whole-document revision;
- Skill 1-guided targeted revision.

## Step 3 — Map academic function

Classify each part as:

- background/context;
- literature synthesis;
- research gap;
- objective/hypothesis;
- methods;
- results;
- interpretation;
- implication;
- limitation;
- conclusion;
- administrative/institutional note.

## Step 4 — Select reporting-guideline family

Use `reporting_guidelines_map.md` when the document is a research manuscript, protocol, review, case report, diagnostic study, qualitative study, or quality-improvement report.

## Step 5 — Revise by priority

Priority order:

1. correct scientific meaning;
2. strengthen method and data traceability;
3. correct unsupported claims;
4. align with reporting expectations;
5. improve paragraph structure;
6. improve language and style.

## Step 6 — Preserve meaning

For each substantial change, ask:

- Did the revision alter the claim?
- Did it add a claim?
- Did it remove a claim?
- Is the new wording supported by supplied evidence?
- Does the author need to verify it?

## Step 7 — Mark unresolved information

Use required markers. Do not complete unknown details from general knowledge.

## Step 8 — Produce accountability output

A full output must include:

- revised text;
- revision log;
- claim traceability;
- unresolved markers;
- author checklist;
- meaning-drift risk;
- submission-readiness status.
