# Health-Science Style Guide — French and English

## General style

Use precise, sober, institutionally appropriate academic language. Avoid inflated claims, generic transitions, excessive promotional language, and vague statements.

## French style

Prefer:

- “Cette étude vise à…”
- “Les résultats suggèrent…”
- “Dans le contexte marocain…”
- “La population cible comprend…”
- “Les données seront recueillies au moyen de…”
- “Les limites attendues concernent…”

Avoid:

- “Il est évident que…”
- “De nos jours…”
- “Cette problématique est très importante…”
- “Les résultats prouvent…” unless design supports proof.

Use “littératie en santé” for health literacy. Do not replace it with “alphabétisation” unless specifically referring to literacy in the narrow reading/writing sense.

## English style

Prefer:

- “This study aims to…”
- “The findings suggest…”
- “In the Moroccan context…”
- “Participants will be recruited from…”
- “Data will be collected using…”
- “The main limitation is likely to be…”

Avoid:

- “In today’s world…”
- “It is very important to note…”
- “This study proves…” unless a strong causal design supports it.
- generic “healthcare is a cornerstone of society” language.

## Discipline-sensitive terminology

- Nursing: patient safety, clinical supervision, scope of practice, care continuity, competence development.
- Anesthesia/critical care: perioperative safety, monitoring, oxygenation, airway management, adverse events, protocols.
- Public health: determinants, coverage, access, equity, prevention, surveillance, health policy.
- Rehabilitation/kinésithérapie: functional limitation, participation, rehabilitation pathway, therapeutic exercise, outcome measures.
- Radiology/imaging: radiation protection, image quality, patient experience, protocol optimization, dose management.
- Laboratory medicine: pre-analytical, analytical, post-analytical phases, quality control, traceability, accreditation.
- Health-science education: clinical learning environment, competency-based education, simulation, assessment, supervision.

## Morocco/institutional tone

For ESSS or Moroccan institutional documents, use formal French and avoid overclaiming. Mention local context only when it is relevant and supported.
