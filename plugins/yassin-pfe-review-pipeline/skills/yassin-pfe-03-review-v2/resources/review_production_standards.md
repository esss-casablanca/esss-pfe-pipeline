# Review-Production Standards (condensed)

The version-producing steps (`Yassin_PFE_03_Review_V2`, `Yassin_PFE_05_Review_V3`) must build to the **same
bar** that `Yassin_PFE_01_Review_V1` sets and that the CA skills appraise against. This is the condensed
checklist of that bar. It does not replace `Yassin_PFE_01_Review_V1`'s full doctrine; it guarantees the
later versions do not drift from it.

## Mandatory elements of any review version

- **Consensus deep search** when available — never skipped; **paced** (batch ≤3, wait on rate limit),
  documented in the search log with run-vs-deferred families (R7). **Run the full ≈12-family deep cycle for EVERY review version (V1, V2, V3), not only the appraisal-flagged gaps.** Inclusion is **worldwide and
  subject-driven (R6)** — region is a discussion lens only. Triangulate further sources or justify their absence (Table B).
- **Forced systematic review type (R5)** — the most rigorous synthesis the question admits (full
  systematic review, meta-analysis, or mixed-methods/observational SR); **never scoping or narrative,
  even if the note de cadrage says so**. Report per PRISMA 2020 with Cochrane/JBI conduct.
- **Explicit eligibility criteria** (with PFE feasibility criteria) and a transparent
  selection/PRISMA-flow narrative. Never invent PRISMA counts.
- **Citation verification** (Table I): verify before using as core evidence; exclude or label
  unverified items; never fabricate identifiers.
- **Study-level extraction** across the required fields (Table D), marked abstract-limited where
  full text was unavailable.
- **Methodological comparison BEFORE synthesis** (Table E): design, sampling, instruments,
  analysis, transferability. Do not merge methodologically incompatible studies as equivalent.
- **Heterogeneity analysis** (Table F) used to justify the synthesis method.
- **Risk of bias / quality** with a design-appropriate tool, judgement, concerns, and
  consequence for synthesis (Table G) — never bare "good/moderate" labels.
- **Integrative synthesis** that answers: what is established, what is uncertain, what is
  consistent or contradictory, who is underrepresented, which tools/definitions dominate, which
  weaknesses recur, why methods explain differences, which gaps are specific, and the PFE
  implication.
- **Certainty/confidence**: GRADE / GRADE-CERQual, or a structured confidence assessment.
- **Specific evidence gaps** (what, where, for whom, why). The review is a **standalone article (R1)** —
  no Component-2 bridge is required (at most a brief 'Implications for practice, education and research').
- **Proportionate claims**: practice/policy/clinical statements never exceed the certainty and
  directness of the evidence.

## Prohibited shortcuts (any present = a Critical defect)

Undocumented search; Consensus skipped when available; summarising Consensus instead of the
studies; unverified citations as core evidence; invented PRISMA numbers; ignored contradictory
evidence; incompatible studies merged as equivalent; meta-analysis on non-comparable data;
overstated implications; presenting a limited scan as a complete systematic review.

## Markers instead of fabrication

When a required value or source does not yet exist, insert a transparent marker rather than a
fabricated value: `[COUNT REQUIRED]`, `[REFERENCE REQUIRED]`, `[DATA REQUIRED]`,
`[METHOD DETAIL REQUIRED]`, `[ETHICS DETAIL REQUIRED]`, `[LOCAL CONTEXT REQUIRED]`,
`[AUTHOR TO VERIFY]`. Surface every marker to the student as an input they must supply.
